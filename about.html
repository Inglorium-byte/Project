  • Mousse

    Description

    Mousse is your whimsical dessert buddy who knows how to keep things light and tasty! Each spoonful feels like a serenade from a sugar-laden symphony, charming your senses and making you grin like you’re in on some delicious secret. It’s not heavy or demanding, oh no, it’s as light as a feather, and just as delightful.

    Ingridients

    • White baking chocolate
    • Heavy whipping cream: Heavy whipping cream aerates the mousse and gives it that distinct creaminess.
    • Sugar
    • Vanilla extract
    • Mixed fresh berries (optional): While optional, the addition of mixed fresh berries creates a lovely and light contrast to the richness of this White Chocolate Mousse.

    Directions

    1. Step 1: Prepare the mousse base.

      In a small heavy saucepan, combine chocolate and 2/3 cup cream. Cook and stir the mixture over medium-low heat until smooth. Transfer it to a large bowl and cool to room temperature.

    2. Step 2: Whip the heavy cream.

      In a small bowl, beat the remaining cream until it begins to thicken. Add sieved and powdered sugar and vanilla. Beat the mixture until soft peaks form.

    3. Step 3: Fold heavy cream into chocolate mixture.

      Fold 1/4 cup whipped cream into the chocolate mixture, then fold in the remaining whipped cream.

    4. Step 4: Spoon into serving dish(es) and chill.

      Refrigerate while covered, for at least two hours. If desired, garnish with berries.

  • Magma Cake

    Description

    Mousse is a delectable treat starts off with an unassuming, rich chocolate exterior. Take your spoon and break through the surface, and suddenly, like a volcano erupting, a river of molten chocolate lava comes rushing out, creating a gooey, luscious mess that's nothing short of magical. This chocolatey eruption doesn't just flow, it dances and twirls, teasing your taste buds with every swirl.

    Ingridients

    • 6 ounces (170g) high-quality semi-sweet chocolate*
    • 1/2 cup (8 Tbsp; 113g) unsalted butter
    • 1/4 cup (31g) all-purpose flour (spooned & leveled)
    • 1/2 cup (60g) sugar (powdered and sieved)
    • 1/8 teaspoon salt
    • 2 large eggs
    • ice cream, berries, and/or chocolate syrup (optional)

    Directions

    1. Step 1: Spray four 6-ounce ramekins with nonstick cooking spray and dust with cocoa powder.
    2. Step 2: Preheat oven to 425°F (218°C).
    3. Step 3: Coarsely chop the chocolate.

      Place butter in a medium heat-proof bowl, then add chopped chocolate on top. Microwave on high in 10-second increments, stirring after each until completely smooth. Set aside.

    4. Step 4: Whisk the flour, confectioners’ sugar, and salt together in a small bowl. Whisk the eggs and egg yolks together until combined in another small bowl.

      Pour the flour mixture and eggs into the bowl of chocolate. Slowly stir everything together using a silicone spatula or wooden spoon. If there are any lumps, gently use your whisk to rid them. The batter will be slightly thick.

    5. Step 5:Spoon chocolate batter evenly into each prepared ramekin or muffin cup.
    6. Step 6:Place ramekins onto a baking sheet and bake for 12–14 minutes until the sides appear solid and firm; the tops will still look soft.
    7. Step 7:Allow to cool for 1 minute, then cover each with an inverted plate and turn over.
    8. Step 8:Add toppings, enjoy!
